DAIRY FARMING

  1. Dairying is an important source of subsidiary income to marginal farmers and agricultural labourers.

  2. The manure from animals provides a good source of organic matter for improving soil fertility and crop yields.

  3. The gober gas from the dung is used as fuel for domestic purposes as also for running engines for drawing water from well.

  4. The surplus fodder and agricultural by-products are gainfully utilised for feeding the animals.

  5. Almost all draught power for farm operations and transportation is supplied by bullocks.

  6. Since agriculture is mostly seasonal, there is a possibility of finding employment throughout the year for many persons through dairy farming.

  7. Thus, dairy also provides employment throughout the year. The main beneficiaries of dairy programmes are small/marginal farmers and landless labourers.

  8. A farmer can earn a gross surplus of about Rs. 12,000 per year from a unit consisting of 2 milking buffaloes.

COMMERCIAL FARMING

  • Commercial farming involves farming a single crop over the entire piece of land for the commercial purpose for say imports and exports of the crops.

  • Growing a wide range of crops and raising animals for food, raw materials, or export are all part of commercial farming.

  • This is done for financial gain.

PLANTATION FARMING

  • Plantation farming is the practice of clearing a large parcel of forest land and planting the desired crops in huge numbers on the cleared land.

  • This type of farming helps in increasing the production of the desired produce and makes it easier to control the cultivation.

COMMERCIAL GRAIN FARMING

  1. Commercial Grain Farming is an extensive agricultural activities whereby crops are grown for commercial purpose.

  2. Most commonly commercial grown grains are wheat and maize.

  3. It is mostly practiced in temperate grasslands of North America, Europe and Asia.

COMMERCIAL MIXED FARMING

  1. Mixed farming: In mixed farming, the land is used for growing food and fodder crops and rearing livestock.

  2. It is practiced in Europe, eastern USA, Argentina, southeast Australia, New Zealand and South Africa.
    Commercial farming: In commercial farming crops are grown and animals are reared for sale in the market.

PRIMITIVE SUBSISTENCE FARMING

  1. Primitive subsistence agriculture is also known as shifting cultivation or slash and burn cultivation.

  2. In this type of farming, a patch of land is cleared and then set on fire.

  3. This patch of land is used to sow seeds and grow crops.
